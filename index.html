Getting Juggy with It!

In the Beginning... There Was a Riot... of Celebration

Michigan’s Wolverines had been playing Minnesota’s Golden Gophers off and on since 1892, but in 1903, this year was different. With 29 straight victories under its belt, Michigan’s “Yost’s machine” squad prepared to take on “Doc” Williams’ veteran, 10-0 Minnesota squad at Northrop Field for the “Championship of the West.”

As the Wolverines arrived in Minneapolis for the big game, Yost had a moment of paranoia. Fearing shenanigans or tampering by their rivals, he ordered Tommy Roberts, a student manager, to purchase a jug for water for his team at a local shop. A five-gallon Red Wing Pottery jug was purchased for 30 cents, and the much-anticipated game began. The teams battled fiercely through a scoreless first half. Michigan scored first, taking a 6-0 lead midway through the second half. With two minutes left in the game, the Gophers crossed the goal line to even the score. Minnesota fans erupted in celebration and rushed the field, which forced referees to call the game—at a tie.

Thanks to the chaos on the field and Michigan’s scramble to catch their train home, Yost’s jug was forgotten, abandoned in Minneapolis. The Gophers’ equipment manager Oscar Munson found it and gave it to Munson. Minnesota’s athletic director, L.J. Cooke decided to decorate it to commemorate the results of the game. They painted the evening’s score (6-6), making Minnesota’s “6” larger than Michigan’s, with the caption “Michigan Jug Captured by Oscar, October 31, 1903.Cooke suggested the teams play for its return. Yost agreed that playing for the jug could make a fine tradition, and the teams prepared for a rematch in Minneapolis on November 20, 1909… the rest is history
